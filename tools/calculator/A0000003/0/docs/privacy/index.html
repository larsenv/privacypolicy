Privacy Policy

Great Calculator is designed to run primarily on your device.

Most calculations happen locally. This policy explains what the app stores, what information may leave your device when you use specific features, and how to manage your choices in the current release.

What Great Calculator stores on your device

The app stores certain information locally so it can remember your setup and preserve committed results between launches.

Preferences and local app state

  • appearance preference, such as light or dark theme
  • haptics preference, when supported by the device
  • number formatting, percent mode, answer format, and angle-unit preferences
  • first-launch onboarding and EULA/welcome acknowledgement state

Committed history and rate cache

  • committed calculator history entries, including calculator type, expression, result, and timestamp
  • a small local cache of recent currency exchange rates used to improve resilience when the device is temporarily offline
  • file-backed history snapshots in the app’s Application Support area for durability

Local storage purpose: This information is stored locally so the app can preserve your preferences, keep history available between launches, and avoid unnecessary repeated currency-rate lookups.

What may leave your device

The current release keeps off-device activity limited and user-initiated.

Live currency exchange-rate lookups

When you use the Money calculator’s currency-conversion mode and request a live rate, the app sends the selected source currency code and destination currency code to the Frankfurter exchange-rate service.

  • The app does not need to send your full calculation history to fetch a rate.
  • The conversion amount is calculated locally after the rate is returned.
  • As with any HTTPS request, the service provider and network infrastructure may receive standard connection metadata such as IP address, timing, and basic technical request information.

Support email you choose to send

If you tap an email support link, the app hands control to your email application using a standard mailto: link. You choose whether to send the message. If you do, we may receive your email address, message content, and any attachments you choose to include.

Apple Ads attribution path

The codebase includes a default-off Apple Ads attribution integration, but the current release keeps the feature disabled. In the current release, the app does not request Apple Ads attribution and does not use the feature for tracking or targeted advertising.

Visits to this public website

If this privacy site is hosted on GitHub Pages, GitHub states that visitor IP addresses are logged and stored for security purposes. That website-host behavior is separate from how the app itself works.

How information is used

App functionality

Preferences and local state are used so the app launches in the same configuration you selected previously.

History and continuity

Committed history is used to let you review, recall, and manage prior calculations from within the app.

Currency conversion

Live rate requests are used only to return the exchange rate necessary to complete the conversion you requested.

Support

If you contact support, we use the information you send to answer your question, troubleshoot the issue, and maintain basic support records.

Third parties and service providers

The current release does not integrate third-party analytics or advertising SDKs. Limited third-party services may still be involved when you intentionally use certain external paths.

Retention and deletion

Data stored locally in the app

Local settings, onboarding flags, history, and cached exchange rates remain on the device until you clear the relevant data in the app, overwrite it through normal use, or uninstall the app.

Support emails

If you email support, correspondence may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to respond, troubleshoot, and keep basic service records, subject to legal obligations.

Your choices and controls

The current release is designed so that most privacy choices are direct and local.

  • Clear history: Use the app’s history controls to remove individual history items or clear all saved history.
  • Avoid live requests: Do not use the live currency-rate lookup if you prefer not to send a one-time exchange-rate request.
  • Do not send support email: You control whether to open your mail app and whether to send a support message.
  • Delete local data: Uninstalling the app removes the app and its sandboxed local storage from your device.

Children’s privacy

The current release is not designed to require personal information from children in order to function. Great Calculator does not require account creation, and most features work entirely on-device. If a child voluntarily sends a support email, the email will be handled like any other support request.

Changes to this policy

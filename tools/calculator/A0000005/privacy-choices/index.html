Privacy Choices

Simple ways to manage privacy in the current Project Countdown release.

Project Countdown is designed so that most privacy controls are direct and local. This page explains the practical choices available in the current release.

1. Delete saved countdowns

Use the app’s event controls to remove individual countdowns or delete groups of local events. This removes the event title, notes, stored date, precision, and any event-specific settings tied to the deleted countdown.

2. Remove a custom background photo

Edit the event and replace the custom background photo or switch back to a non-photo background. When a saved photo is no longer referenced by any event, the app removes the associated local photo copy.

3. Turn history packs on or off

The app stores which curated history packs are enabled. If you do not want a pack’s curated milestones in your local timeline, disable that pack in the app’s history-pack controls.

4. Avoid or revoke photo-library access

If you prefer not to grant photo access, do not choose a custom background photo. If you already granted access and want to revoke it, change the permission in iOS / iPadOS Settings for Project Countdown.

5. Choose whether to send support email

The app uses a standard mail link for support. You decide whether to open your mail app and whether to send a message, screenshots, or attachments.

6. Remove local app data

Uninstalling the app removes the app and its sandboxed local storage from the device itself, including local countdowns, preferences, enabled history-pack state, and saved photo copies stored by the app.

No account-deletion workflow is needed

The current release does not create or require user accounts.

Because the app does not provide account creation or sign-in, there is no in-app account deletion flow for the current release. If that changes in a future release, the app and this site should be updated before release.

No account No cloud profile No cross-app tracking

What this page does and does not change

  • It helps you understand your options: delete countdowns, remove photos, turn history packs off, avoid optional permissions, avoid support email, or remove the app.
  • It does not create new data flows: visiting this page does not change how the app itself processes data.
  • It does not add tracking: the current release keeps Apple Ads attribution disabled and does not include third-party analytics SDKs.

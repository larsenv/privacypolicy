Privacy Policy

Project Countdown is designed to keep your countdown data primarily on your device.

Core countdown features work locally. This policy explains what the app stores, what information may leave your device when you choose specific features, and how to manage your choices in the current release.

What Project Countdown stores on your device

Preferences and local app state

  • selected view mode, theme, and background style
  • first-launch onboarding and EULA acceptance state
  • whether you created a custom event and which history packs are enabled

Countdown events and timeline data

  • event title, optional notes/comments, event date, and whether the event is date-only or includes a specific time
  • visual choices such as background style and optional bundled image references
  • a local recovery copy of the event store used to help restore data if the primary event file is damaged

Optional background photos

  • if you choose a custom photo, the selected image is converted to an app-optimized local copy and stored in the app’s private sandbox
  • the original photo-library item identifier is used only during selection to complete the import safely

What may leave your device

Support email you choose to send

If you tap an email support link, the app hands control to your email application using a standard mailto: link. You decide whether to send the message. If you do, we may receive your email address, message content, screenshots, and any attachments you choose to include.

Visits to this public website

If this site is hosted on GitHub Pages, GitHub says visitor IP addresses are logged and stored for security purposes. That website-host behavior is separate from how the app itself works.

Apple Ads attribution path

The codebase includes a default-off Apple Ads attribution integration, but the current release keeps the feature disabled. In the current release, the app does not request Apple Ads attribution and does not use the feature for tracking or targeted advertising.

How information is used

App functionalityLocal settings and event data are used so the app launches in the same configuration you selected and can render your countdowns, grid, and timeline views.
PersonalizationOptional notes, chosen themes, enabled history packs, and custom background photos are used to personalize the app experience on your device.
ResilienceA local backup copy of the event store helps the app recover if the primary event file becomes unavailable or corrupted.
SupportIf you contact support, we use the information you send to answer your question, troubleshoot the issue, and keep basic support records.

Retention and deletion

Local data in the app

Local settings, countdowns, notes, enabled history packs, and optional saved background photos remain on the device until you change or delete them in the app, replace them through normal use, or uninstall the app.

Optional photo copies

If you remove a custom background photo, replace it, or delete the event that references it, the app removes the associated local photo copy when it is no longer needed.

Device backups

Depending on your Apple backup settings, local app data may also be included in device backups managed by Apple. Those backups are controlled by your Apple account and backup configuration rather than a Project Countdown server.

Support emails

If you email support, correspondence may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to respond, troubleshoot, and keep basic service records, subject to legal obligations.

Your choices and controls

  • Delete countdowns: use the app’s event controls to remove one or more saved countdowns.
  • Remove notes or a custom photo: edit the event and remove the note text or replace the background photo.
  • Avoid photo access: do not choose a custom background photo, or revoke photo access in iOS / iPadOS Settings if you no longer want the app to access your library.
  • Turn history packs off: disable a curated history pack in the app’s history pack controls if you do not want its events in your local timeline.
  • Do not send support email: you control whether to open your mail app and whether to send a support message.
  • Delete local app data: uninstalling the app removes the app and its sandboxed local storage from the device itself.

No account-deletion workflow is needed in the current release because the app does not create or require user accounts.

Children’s privacy

The current release is not designed to require personal information from children in order to function. Project Countdown does not require account creation, and its core countdown features work locally on-device. If a child voluntarily sends a support email, the email is handled like any other support request.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy when the app’s privacy practices change, when functionality changes, or when legal or platform requirements change. The effective date at the top of this page will be updated when a material revision is published.

Privacy Policy

Project Milepost is local-first, with clear disclosure for ads and purchases.

This policy describes Project Milepost version 1.4, build 40, for iPhone and iPad. It explains what is kept on your device, how the guided tour works locally, what may be handled by Google for ads, what is handled by Apple for purchases, and how to manage your choices.

Ad reports

If you see an inappropriate or age-inappropriate ad, open Project Milepost and use Menu > Ads > Report an Ad. Include the approximate date and time, your country or region, a short description, and a screenshot if possible.

Plain-language summary

No Project Milepost account

The app does not require sign-in and does not maintain a Project Milepost account record for you.

Your personal events stay local

Event names, dates, notes, settings, selected Calendar imports, and optional photo copies are stored on your device for the app experience.

Ads use Google AdMob

The free app may show Google AdMob banner ads unless Remove Ads is active. Google may receive ad-request information needed to provide, limit, secure, measure, and improve ads.

Purchases use Apple

The one-time Remove Ads purchase is handled by Apple. Project Milepost checks Apple purchase status so it can hide ads after purchase or restore.

Local app data

Information stored on your device.

Project Milepost stores the information needed to provide your event timeline and personalization features. This information is not sent to a Project Milepost server.

Events and countdowns

Event names, dates, date-only or date-and-time precision, optional notes, source type, local duplicate-prevention information, and view-related event details.

Calendar import copies

If you choose Calendar import, the app reads Calendar events only after permission and only for the preview window. When you select events, Project Milepost stores a local copy of the selected title, date and time details, all-day status, Calendar name, and Calendar identifiers used to prevent duplicate imports. The current import flow does not write changes back to Apple Calendar and does not keep background sync running.

Optional photos

If you choose an event background photo, Project Milepost stores an app-optimized local copy so the event can display that image.

Settings and preferences

View mode, appearance preferences, 12-hour or 24-hour time preference, onboarding and license acknowledgement, enabled history selections, and other app settings.

Local recovery data

The app may keep local recovery copies or shared local snapshots to preserve event data and support the app widget. These copies remain inside the device’s app storage.

Support email you send

If you email support, the email address you use and the message details you provide are used to respond to your request.

Advertising through Google AdMob

Project Milepost may show a compact Google AdMob banner near the top of the main app screen unless Remove Ads is active. Google AdMob and its advertising partners may process information from the app to provide ads, limit repeated ads, detect fraud or abuse, measure ad performance, improve ad services, and comply with law.

Examples of advertising informationInternet address, approximate location inferred from that address, device or app identifiers, advertising activity, ad interactions, performance information, and diagnostic information such as crash or error data.
When advertising startsThe app checks purchase status and privacy requirements before requesting ads. If Remove Ads is active, banner ads are hidden.
Privacy choicesWhen Google provides a choices form for your region and settings, it is available inside the app at Menu > Ads > Ad Privacy Choices.
No Project Milepost ad serverProject Milepost does not operate its own advertising server. Advertising is provided by Google AdMob.
Authorized seller fileThe public Google authorized seller file is available at https://sophiadetavex.github.io/privacypolicy/app-ads.txt.

Important: because Google AdMob is included, App Store privacy answers for Project Milepost must disclose Google’s advertising data practices. The app listing must not state that the app has no advertising.

Purchases through Apple

Remove Ads is a one-time purchase handled by Apple. Project Milepost does not receive your payment-card details. The app receives Apple-provided purchase status so it can unlock Remove Ads, restore the purchase, or update access if Apple reports a change.

  • Purchase name: Remove Ads.
  • Purchase value: com.projectmilepost.removeads.
  • Intended United States price: $0.99.
  • Purpose: hide Google AdMob banner ads after Apple confirms the purchase.

Permissions

CalendarRequested only when you start Calendar import. The app needs read access to preview events for selection. Project Milepost does not write back to Calendar.
PhotosRequested only when you choose a custom event background photo. You can use the app without granting photo access.
Tracking permissionProject Milepost may ask Apple tracking permission before ad tracking. Advertising privacy choices are handled through Google’s ad choices flow when available.
Permissions not usedThe current release does not request camera, microphone, contacts, precise location, health, or notification permission.

Data sharing and protection

Project Milepost shares user data only as needed for the features described in this policy: Google AdMob for advertising, Apple for App Store purchases and app distribution, the device operating system for Calendar and photo permissions, and email providers when you choose to email support. Third parties that receive user data through the app are expected to provide the same or equal protection required by this policy, Apple’s app rules, and applicable law.

Project Milepost does not sell your personal event records. The app does not send your personal events, notes, photos, or Calendar data to an artificial intelligence service.

Retention and deletion

How long information is kept and how to remove it.

Local app data

Events, notes, preferences, local photo copies, history selections, and local recovery copies remain until you delete them in the app, change the setting, or delete the app from your device.

Calendar and photo permission

You can revoke Calendar or photo permission in iOS Settings. Revoking permission prevents future access but does not automatically delete local event copies already saved in Project Milepost.

Advertising choices

Use Menu > Ads > Ad Privacy Choices when available. You may also manage advertising-related choices through Apple device settings and Google account or ad settings where applicable.

Support email deletion

Email trentshurts@gmail.com to request deletion of support messages you sent. Include enough information to locate the message.

Website visitors

This website is a public information site. If it is hosted on GitHub Pages or another hosting provider, that provider may process visitor information such as internet address, browser information, logs, or security events to operate and protect the website. That host-level processing is separate from the Project Milepost app.

Children and sensitive information

Project Milepost is a general milestone and date-tracking app. It is not submitted as an app primarily for children and should not be marketed as being for children. The app does not include social networking, public posting, user-to-user messaging, gambling, health-data features, finance-data features, or user-generated public content.

Changes to this policy

If Project Milepost’s data practices change, this policy and App Store privacy answers should be updated before or at the time the changed app is submitted. The effective date above identifies the current policy version.

Contact

For privacy or support questions, email trentshurts@gmail.com.
